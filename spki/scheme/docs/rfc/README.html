Library of Cyberspace

The Soup. The Realm. The Seal.

What Is This?

Imagine a library where:

  • Your work is safe - because friends keep copies for each other
  • Nothing gets lost - even if one computer fails
  • You know who made what - like a signature on a painting
  • It belongs to everyone - like a community garden

That's the Library of Cyberspace.

Why Does This Matter?

We all have things worth keeping: family photos, creative work, research, code, things we want to pass on.

Right now, most of this lives on company servers. That works great... until the company shuts down, your account gets locked, or something just disappears.

Friends don't disappear. Friends keep copies.

The Simple Idea

What if preserving things worked like borrowing books from friends?

    You <----> Friend <----> Friend
     |           |             |
     +-----------+-------------+
                 |
      Everyone helps everyone.
      Like a neighborhood library,
      but for anything digital.

That's really all this is.

Our Promise to Each Other

  • We keep copies - so nothing is lost
  • We verify authenticity - so nothing is changed
  • We share generously - because that's what friends do
  • We help each other - because together we're stronger

A Newton for Cyberspace

In 1993, Apple made something called the Newton. It had a revolutionary idea: your stuff just exists. You don't save files or open documents. Things float in a soup of objects, and you find them by asking questions.

The Newton died because it needed a network that didn't exist yet. We have that network now.

We're building the Newton it deserves: a soup that spans friends, objects that prove themselves, identity without permission, memory that can't be rewritten.

Cyberspace is their synthesis: local-first realms, federated via capabilities, with agents swimming in the soup.
